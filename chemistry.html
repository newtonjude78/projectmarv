3. STANDARD SEPARATION TECHNIQUES FOR MIXTURES

Separation techniques are physical methods used for separating the components of various mixtures.

The principle of separation is based on the differences in the physical properties of the constituents of the mixtures which include:

  1. Physical state (solid liquid or gas)
  2. Particle size
  3. Colour
  4. density
  5. melting point
  6. boiling point
  7. magnetic property
  8. solubility

Common Separation Techniques

  1. Filtration: Filtration method is used to separate a mixture of an insoluble solid from a liquid. The method is based on the differences in the physical state and the insolubility of one compo- nent of the mixture in the other. The following mixtures can be separated by filtra- tion method: • Suspended particles in a liquid or muddy water; Sediments in a sample of water, • A saturated solution in contact with undissolved solid.
